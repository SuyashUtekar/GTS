3. Wrong Interpretations
One notable wrong interpretation. The summary states the 20% single counterparty limit applies generically to NBFCs “excluding gold loan companies.” That is directionally correct, but the document’s framing is that 20% is the standard LEF single-counterparty ceiling applicable to all non-exempt counterparties including most NBFCs — it is not specifically a unique NBFC-only limit. The special NBFC-specific limits (7.5%/12.5% for gold-loan NBFCs, 25% for connected NBFC groups) are correctly called out. Also, the summary does not clarify that the 7.5% gold-loan NBFC limit is measured against capital funds (Tier I + Tier II), not eligible capital base — a meaningful definitional difference the document explicitly draws.

4. Keyword Coverage
Partially covered — several important keywords missing. The summary covers: eligible capital base, connected counterparties, G-SIB, NBFC, capital market exposure, net worth, board-approved policies, Basel, CRM, large exposures, reporting. However, it misses: Intra-Group Transactions and Exposures (ITEs), Look-Through Approach (LTA), QCCP (Qualifying Central Counterparty), Country Risk, Inter-Bank Liabilities (IBL), NOFHC, economic interdependence, solo vs. consolidated level, credit conversion factors (CCFs), and Tier 1 eligible capital base as a distinct concept. These are substantive themes running through multiple chapters and their absence is a meaningful gap.

Overall Verdict: The summary is a reasonable starting point but is best described as a partial, limits-focused précis rather than a complete summary. It is factually sound on the numbers it cites, with one G-SIB nuance that could mislead a reader, and it leaves out roughly 40% of the document’s substantive content.​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​​
