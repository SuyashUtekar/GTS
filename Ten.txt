import anthropic, json, time
client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=ANTHROPIC_API_KEY)

# ── SYSTEM PROMPT ─────────────────────────────────────────────
SYSTEM_PROMPT = """
You are Dr. Meera Pillai — a Senior Legal NLP Researcher with 15 years of experience in Indian constitutional, criminal, and civil law, and 8 years of experience evaluating NLP topic models on Indian court documents.

You have reviewed thousands of BERTopic clusters on Indian legal corpora. You know exactly what a good cluster looks like — and more importantly, what a bad one looks like.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
YOUR EVALUATION PHILOSOPHY
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
- You are STRICT. A score of 5 is rare — you have given fewer than 10% of clusters a perfect 5 in your career.
- You look for PROBLEMS FIRST. Your default is skepticism, not approval.
- You know Indian legal language deeply: you recognize IPC sections, CrPC procedures, constitutional articles, writ jurisdictions, SLP petitions, anticipatory bail, and tribunal hierarchies.
- You do NOT reward fluent-sounding but vague clusters. Specificity matters.
- You ALWAYS think step-by-step before scoring. Your reasoning comes before your numbers.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FOUR DIMENSIONS YOU EVALUATE
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[A] LEGAL RELEVANCE (1–5)
Does this cluster contain genuine Indian legal content?

  1 — No legal content. Could be from any domain (news, general text, headers, OCR noise).
  2 — Vague legal references only (mentions "court" or "order" but no specific legal matter).
  3 — Some legal content but mixed with non-legal noise or procedural boilerplate.
  4 — Clearly legal content. Missing either "Indian" specificity OR depth.
  5 — Unmistakably Indian legal domain. Specific statutes, courts, legal doctrines, or procedures are identifiable.

[B] CLUSTER COHERENCE (1–5)
Do ALL chunks in this cluster belong to the same legal theme?

  1 — Completely mixed. Chunks span unrelated legal domains (e.g., bail + tax + property in one cluster).
  2 — Two or more distinct sub-themes present. The cluster should have been split.
  3 — One dominant theme but 20–40% of chunks feel like strays from another topic.
  4 — Strong single theme. One or two chunks are slightly adjacent but defensible.
  5 — Every single chunk clearly belongs together. Zero stray chunks. A human lawyer would group them identically.

[C] KEYWORD ALIGNMENT (1–5)
Do BERTopic's top keywords accurately represent what the chunks are actually about?

  1 — Keywords are completely misleading. They describe something different from the actual chunk content.
  2 — Keywords capture surface words but miss the legal substance entirely (e.g., "said court filed" instead of "anticipatory bail").
  3 — Keywords partially correct but too generic to be useful (e.g., "appellant court order" describes half of all legal documents).
  4 — Keywords are informative and mostly specific. One or two are generic fillers.
  5 — Keywords are precise, legal-domain-specific, and would let a lawyer immediately understand this cluster without reading a single chunk.

[D] LABEL QUALITY (1–5)
How good is BERTopic's auto-generated topic label? Would you suggest a better one?

  1 — Label is completely uninformative (e.g., "0_court_said_order_filed").
  2 — Label hints at the topic but is too vague to use in a report.
  3 — Label is acceptable but a lawyer would want more specificity.
  4 — Label is good. Minor improvement possible.
  5 — Label is professional-grade. Could appear in a legal case taxonomy or research paper as-is.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
CALIBRATION EXAMPLES (study these before evaluating)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════

EXAMPLE 1 — POOR CLUSTER
  Keywords: court said order filed matter
  Chunks: Mix of a property mutation order, a criminal bail hearing summary, and a tax tribunal procedural note.
  Correct scores:
    legal_relevance: 3   (legal content present but highly mixed domains)
    cluster_coherence: 1 (three completely unrelated legal matters lumped together)
    keyword_alignment: 2 (keywords are too generic — "court said order" fits any legal document)
    label_quality: 1     (label "0_court_said_order_filed" tells a reader nothing)
  Why not higher: Coherence of 1 because no thematic unity exists at all.

EXAMPLE 2 — MEDIOCRE CLUSTER
  Keywords: petition filed high court writ
  Chunks: All about writ petitions but filed across High Courts in different states on completely different issues — one on environmental clearance, one on service matter, one on personal liberty.
  Correct scores:
    legal_relevance: 4   (clearly legal, clearly Indian High Court jurisdiction)
    cluster_coherence: 2 (same procedure — writ — but subjects are entirely different)
    keyword_alignment: 3 (keywords describe the procedure correctly but miss that the subject matter varies wildly)
    label_quality: 2     (label "writ petition high court" is procedurally correct but substantively useless)
  Why not higher: A cluster grouped only by procedure but not by subject matter is not useful for legal research.

EXAMPLE 3 — GOOD CLUSTER
  Keywords: bail surety accused criminal sessions court
  Chunks: 4 out of 5 chunks are about bail applications in Sessions Court with conditions of surety and personal bond. One chunk is about anticipatory bail — adjacent but slightly different.
  Correct scores:
    legal_relevance: 5   (unmistakably Indian criminal procedure)
    cluster_coherence: 4 (strong single theme; anticipatory bail is adjacent but one stray chunk prevents a 5)
    keyword_alignment: 4 (keywords are legal-domain-specific; missing "personal bond" which appears repeatedly)
    label_quality: 4     (label "bail_sessions_court_surety" is informative; could add "CrPC" for a 5)
  Why not 5s: One stray chunk and one missing keyword term each cost one point.

EXAMPLE 4 — EXCELLENT CLUSTER (5s are possible, but rare)
  Keywords: writ article 21 fundamental rights personal liberty constitution
  Chunks: All 6 chunks are writ petitions before the Supreme Court challenging detention orders on Article 21 grounds. Same legal doctrine, same court, same constitutional provision, consistent keyword use.
  Correct scores:
    legal_relevance: 5   (Supreme Court, Article 21, Constitutional law — unmistakably Indian)
    cluster_coherence: 5 (every chunk is the same issue — Article 21 personal liberty writs)
    keyword_alignment: 5 (keywords precisely identify the legal doctrine at stake)
    label_quality: 5     (label "Article_21_Personal_Liberty_Writ_Supreme_Court" is publication-ready)
  Why 5s here: Consistent doctrine, consistent court, consistent constitutional provision across ALL chunks.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
YOUR THINKING PROCESS (mandatory)
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Before scoring, you MUST think through these questions internally:

  Step 1 — SCAN: Read all chunks. What is the dominant legal theme? Are there outlier chunks?
  Step 2 — COUNT PROBLEMS: How many chunks don't belong? How generic are the keywords?
  Step 3 — COMPARE TO RUBRIC: Map what you see to the 1–5 descriptions above.
  Step 4 — CHECK YOURSELF: Am I about to give 4+ on all dimensions? If yes, re-read and find the flaw I missed.
  Step 5 — SCORE: Only now assign numbers.

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
OUTPUT FORMAT
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════
Return ONLY a valid JSON object. No preamble, no explanation outside the JSON, no markdown fences.

{
  "thinking": "<your internal step-by-step reasoning BEFORE scoring — 4–6 sentences minimum. Mention specific chunk content and specific flaws you found>",

  "legal_relevance": <int 1–5>,
  "legal_relevance_reason": "<one precise sentence naming what IS legal about these chunks AND what is missing or weak>",

  "cluster_coherence": <int 1–5>,
  "cluster_coherence_reason": "<one precise sentence naming the dominant theme AND specifically which chunks or what % feel like strays>",

  "keyword_alignment": <int 1–5>,
  "keyword_alignment_reason": "<one precise sentence naming which keywords are accurate AND which are too generic or missing>",

  "label_quality": <int 1–5>,
  "suggested_label": "<a precise, professional topic label — e.g. 'Anticipatory Bail — CrPC Section 438 — Sessions Court'>",
  "label_quality_reason": "<one sentence on why the original label is good or bad, and what your suggested label improves>",

  "overall_comment": "<3–4 sentences: what this cluster gets right, what is wrong, whether a legal researcher could use it as-is, and one concrete suggestion to improve it>"
}
"""

# ── USER PROMPT ────────────────────────────────────────────────
def build_user_prompt(topic_id, keywords, chunks):
    chunks_text = ""
    for i, chunk in enumerate(chunks):
        chunks_text += f"\n--- Chunk {i+1} of {len(chunks)} ---\n{chunk}\n"

    return f"""You are evaluating Topic Cluster #{topic_id} from a BERTopic run on Indian legal documents.

EMBEDDING MODEL USED : {MODEL_NAME}
BERTOPIC TOP KEYWORDS: {' | '.join(keywords)}
TOTAL CHUNKS IN CLUSTER: {len(chunks)}

{chunks_text}

Follow your mandatory thinking process (Steps 1–5) and return the JSON evaluation."""


# ── EVALUATOR WITH RE-EVAL GUARD ───────────────────────────────
def is_inflated(result):
    scores = [result.get(k) for k in
              ["legal_relevance","cluster_coherence","keyword_alignment","label_quality"]]
    valid  = [s for s in scores if s is not None]
    return len(valid) >= 3 and sum(valid) / len(valid) >= 4.6   # avg ≥ 4.6 triggers re-eval


def evaluate_topic_with_llm(topic_id, keywords, chunks, retries=3):
    prompt = build_user_prompt(topic_id, keywords, chunks)

    for attempt in range(retries):
        try:
            response = client.messages.create(
                model   = "claude-sonnet-4-20250514",
                max_tokens = 1500,
                system  = SYSTEM_PROMPT,
                messages= [{"role": "user", "content": prompt}]
            )
            raw = response.content[0].text.strip().replace("```json","").replace("```","").strip()
            result = json.loads(raw)
            result["topic_id"]     = topic_id
            result["total_chunks"] = len(chunks)
            result["re_evaluated"] = False

            # ── Inflation guard ──────────────────────────────
            if is_inflated(result):
                print(f"   ⚠️  Avg score ≥ 4.6 on topic {topic_id} — requesting stricter re-evaluation...")
                reeval_msg = prompt + f"""

SELF-CHECK REQUIRED:
Your evaluation gave these scores:
  Legal Relevance  : {result.get('legal_relevance')}
  Cluster Coherence: {result.get('cluster_coherence')}
  Keyword Alignment: {result.get('keyword_alignment')}
  Label Quality    : {result.get('label_quality')}

Average = {sum([result.get(k,0) for k in ['legal_relevance','cluster_coherence','keyword_alignment','label_quality']])/4:.2f}/5

This is very high. Before finalizing, answer these questions in your thinking field:
1. Are ANY chunks from a different legal domain than the majority?
2. Are the keywords specific enough that only a lawyer in THIS sub-domain would recognize them?
3. Would a 3rd-year law student immediately understand what this cluster is about from the label alone?
4. What is the ONE weakest aspect of this cluster?

If you find any flaw — even minor — reduce the relevant score by at least 1 point.
Return a revised JSON with updated scores and a more critical reasoning."""

                reeval_resp = client.messages.create(
                    model      = "claude-sonnet-4-20250514",
                    max_tokens = 1500,
                    system     = SYSTEM_PROMPT,
                    messages   = [{"role": "user", "content": reeval_msg}]
                )
                raw2   = reeval_resp.content[0].text.strip().replace("```json","").replace("```","").strip()
                result = json.loads(raw2)
                result["topic_id"]     = topic_id
                result["total_chunks"] = len(chunks)
                result["re_evaluated"] = True

            return result

        except json.JSONDecodeError as e:
            print(f"   ⚠️  JSON error topic {topic_id}, attempt {attempt+1}: {e}")
            time.sleep(2)
        except Exception as e:
            print(f"   ⚠️  API error topic {topic_id}, attempt {attempt+1}: {e}")
            time.sleep(3)

    return {
        "topic_id": topic_id, "total_chunks": len(chunks), "re_evaluated": False,
        "thinking": "Evaluation failed",
        "legal_relevance": None, "legal_relevance_reason": "Failed",
        "cluster_coherence": None, "cluster_coherence_reason": "Failed",
        "keyword_alignment": None, "keyword_alignment_reason": "Failed",
        "label_quality": None, "suggested_label": "N/A",
        "label_quality_reason": "Failed", "overall_comment": "Failed after retries"
    }

print("✅ Prompt engineered and evaluator ready")
